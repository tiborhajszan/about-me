About Me

I belong to a generation that grew up with Commodore computers, DOS operating systems, BASIC and FORTRAN programming languages, machine coding - and no internet. . . (imagine that!). Yet, despite starting out as a computer enthusiast, I spent my last 25 years doing biomedical research.

I became known in the field of neuroscience for my pioneering research on the "synaptogenic hypothesis" of major depressive disorder, that is now used to develop highly effective antidepressant medications. Research gave me the privilege to work in distiguished neuroscience laboratories and experience vibrant and inspiring cultures around the World.

It struck me over the years that academia became an industry with the objective of churning out research papers like a factory of toilet brushes. I realized that in order to grow and advance my career, I must move on and, inspired by my childhood dreams, try my luck in the technology sector, where progress and innovation actually happens.

Fortunately, science and computing are closely related animals. The job of a scientist is collecting data, running statistics, and publishing results, which is not far from what a data scientist or a web publisher does. I believe that combining science and computing skills will greatly help me in accomplishing what my mission-statement declares: Helping the millions out there who suffer from major depressive disorder.
